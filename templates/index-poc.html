A Global Health Problem

According to data collected by the CDC, NIH, WHO, and other medical research organizations, pneumonia is a treatable disease if there is proper access to quick and efficient medical care. Although your chances of survival are incredibly high, the data shows that pneumonia remains a major cause of death around the world.

Based on global statistics, pneumonia is especially deadly in countries with low GDP per capita. This is likely due to not having the proper medical resources to avoid getting sick in the first place and the tools necessary for effective treatment.

The research points to the need for an easily accessible way to identify pneumonia and speed up access to necessary treatments, which is the primary motivation of our pneumonia detection project.

The Misdiagnosis Problem

While the prevelancy of pneumonia across the U.S. can't be ignored, there's a second issue to consider. How many individuals are misdiagnosed with pneumonia each year?

According to this article in ScienceDaily, patients are often "misdiagnosed with pneumonia at an alarming rate," leading to an overuse of antibiotics and an increase in healthcare costs.

Pneumonia is second only to congestive heart failure "as the reason for readmission within 30 days of a previous hospitalization," according to researchers led by Henry Ford Infectious Diseases physicians Hiren Pokharna, M.D., and Norman Markowitz, M.D. This study found that "72 percent of patients were misdiagnosed with pneumonia upon readmission to the same hospital." Most of these misdiagnoses happened in the emergency department.

Dr. Pokharna noted that this analysis "points to the importance of using X-ray for ruling out pneumonia."

Both of these problems warrant an immediate solution. How can the medical community diagnose pneumonia quickly and accurately, and also prevent an influx of misdiagnoses?

Enter machine learning.

Machine Learning: A Solution for Improving Diagnosis Rates

Imagine a pneumonia prediction tool more accurate than the human eye.

A fast and accurate resource for analyzing x-rays has the potential to drastically improve pneumonia diagnosis rates while simultaneously reducing the chances of a misdiagnosis.

Machine learning provides a solution to this problem.

A deep learning model is capable of reading x-ray images and classifying them as pneumonia or normal. Even better, the accuracy rate of the model can be measured and improved before releasing it for public use. Our model achieved an accuracy score greater than 90% in the validation phase.
